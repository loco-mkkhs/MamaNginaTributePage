Mama Ngina Kenyatta

The wife of The First President of Kenya who liberated Kenyans from colonialists

Ngina Kenyatta, also referred to as "Mama Ngina," is the widow of Kenya's first president, Jomo Kenyatta, and the mother of Uhuru Kenyatta, who presided over Kenya from 2013 until 2022 as its fourth president. She has been affiliated with the illustrious political family of Kenya and has made a significant contribution to its history. Here is a summary of her life and accomplishments: On June 24, 1933, Ngina Kenyatta was born as Ngina Muhoho in Ngenda, Kiambu, British Kenya. In 1951, at the age of 19, she wed Jomo Kenyatta. After Kenya gained independence from British colonial authority on December 12, 1963, Jomo Kenyatta, a significant figure in the independence movement, was elected as the country's first president. Mama Ngina Kenyatta was a key figure in the struggle for Kenya's independence. She stood by her husband's side both throughout Kenya's declaration of independence and the tumultuous years that followed. Even though Kenyatta was a polygamist, Mama Ngina assumed the duties of the first lady. Ngina Kenyatta held the position of First Lady of Kenya from December 1964 to August 1978. She did so despite losing her husband in 1978. When Jomo Kenyatta was succeeded by Daniel Arap Moi as president, she was still referred to as the First Lady. The mother of Ngina is a devoted Catholic. The influence of Mama Ngina Kenyatta goes beyond her position as first lady. She is regarded as the matriarch of the Kenyatta family, which controls a huge economic empire with an estimated value of millions of dollars. Shares of Kenya's Commercial Bank of Africa, Brookside Dairy, Heritage Hotels East Africa, and Media Max Company are among the companies owned by the Kenyatta family. They also possess thousands of acres of valuable land that Jomo Kenyatta bought in Kenya throughout the 1960s and 1970s. Furthermore, offshore accounts in tax havens like Panama and the British Virgin Islands have been connected to Mama Ngina Kenyatta and her sons, including Uhuru Kenyatta. With rumours of vast property ownership, involvement in the charcoal trade, and other economic endeavours, the family's wealth has drawn attention.Mama Ngina Kenyatta's name has been connected to numerous initiatives and famous locations lately. For instance, the Mama Ngina Waterfront Park in Mombasa, which bears her name, has benefited from large investments for revitalization. The park seeks to provide entertainment, retail, leisure activities, and tourism promotion in the area. Mama Ngina Kenyatta will still be a significant influence in Kenya's political and financial environment when she turns 90 on June 24, 2023. She is a significant figure in Kenyan society due to her historical contributions to the nation and her status as the family matriarch of the Kenyatta clan.
